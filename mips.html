MIPS CPU

MIPS is a "reduced instruction set" architecture popular in embedded systems and other applications needing lightweight CPUs (Patterson and Hennessy, 4th ed.).  For our final project in a senior-level course in computer architecture, I had the opportunity to implement a MIPS CPU.  Even though it was a hardware project, it pushed my engineering skills to their limit, forcing me to develop my ability to manage complexity and debug perplexing problems on the hardware.  By carefully partitioning the architecture into 20 separate modules working in a hierarchy, I was able to hunt down bugs by traversing the hierarchy from the bottom up and verifying that each module worked along the way.

To demonstrate the functionality of the CPU, I programmed the popular game "Snake" in MIPS assembly code and ran it on the processor.
